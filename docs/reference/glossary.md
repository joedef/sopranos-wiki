# Glossary

Mafia terms, Italian-American dialect, and the show's own vocabulary.

---

## The organization

**Boss** — Head of a family. In the show: Ercole DiMeo (imprisoned), then Jackie Aprile Sr. (acting), then [Junior](../characters/junior-soprano.md) (nominal), then [Tony](../characters/tony-soprano.md).

**Underboss** — Second in command.

**Consigliere** — Counselor to the boss, outside the chain of command. [Silvio Dante](../characters/silvio-dante.md) holds it for most of the series.

**Caporegime** (*capo*) — A captain running a crew of soldiers in a territory.

**Soldier** (*soldato*) — A formally initiated member at the base of the structure.

**Associate** — Works with the family without being a member. Non-Italians can only ever be associates — which is why **Hesh Rabkin**, one of Tony's most trusted advisors, can never be made.

**Made** — Formally initiated. *Getting your button.* The show depicts a ceremony for [Christopher](../characters/christopher-moltisanti.md) in season three.

**The Commission** — The governing body of the American Mafia, largely offstage here; the show's inter-family politics run through [Johnny Sack](../characters/johnny-sack.md) and New York instead.

**This thing of ours** (*cosa nostra*) — What members call it. Nobody in the show says "the Mafia" about themselves without irony.

## The business

**Earner** — Someone who generates money. The only quality that reliably buys protection, which is why [Ralph](../characters/ralph-cifaretto.md) and [Vito](../characters/vito-spatafore.md) survive as long as they do.

**Bust-out** — Taking over a legitimate business, maxing its credit lines, stripping the assets, and burning it. The clearest demonstration is Davey Scatino's sporting goods store in "Bust Out" (S2E23).

**No-show job** — A payroll position requiring no work, used to launder money and reward loyalty.

**The skim / the envelope** — Cash tribute passed up the chain.

**Vig** — Interest on a loan-shark debt.

**Sit-down** — A mediated meeting to resolve a dispute.

**Going to the mattresses** — Going to war. The crew does it in "The Blue Comet" (S6E85).

**Whacked / clipped** — Killed. **Rat / flipped** — Informant.

## Italian-American dialect

The show's most underrated accuracy. These are **Southern Italian dialect forms** — largely Neapolitan and Sicilian — worn down by a century in New Jersey, and the pronunciations are genuine rather than invented.

The pattern is consistent: **final vowels drop, and initial consonants soften** (c→g, p→b, t→d).

| As said | From | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **gabagool** | *capicola* | Cured pork shoulder. The show's most famous word. |
| **manigott** | *manicotti* | Pasta |
| **mutzadell** | *mozzarella* | Cheese |
| **prosciutt'** | *prosciutto* | Ham |
| **ricott'** | *ricotta* | Cheese |
| **braciol'** | *braciola* | Rolled meat — also, crudely, slang |
| **agita** | *acidità* | Heartburn; anxiety. Tony has it constantly. |
| **goomar / comare** | *cumare* | Mistress |
| **goombah / compare** | *cumpà* | Close friend; associate |
| **stugots** | *sti cazzi* | Vulgar. Also the name of Tony's boat. |
| **oobatz / pazzo** | *u' pazzu* | Crazy |
| **medigan'** | *americano* | A non-Italian American — mildly contemptuous |
| **skeevy** | *schifare* | Disgusting |
| **cent'anni** | — | "A hundred years" — a toast |

The linguistic point matters: these words are a **fossil record**. They preserve regional forms that have themselves changed or vanished in Italy, carried over by immigrants around 1900 and frozen. When [Paulie](../characters/paulie-walnuts.md) goes to Naples in "Commendatori" and nobody recognizes his food vocabulary, that's the joke. See [Italian-American Identity](../analysis/italian-american-identity.md).

## The show's own vocabulary

**"Oh"** — Not a word, a greeting. Delivered as a full sentence, usually across a room, and doing the work of *hello, I acknowledge your existence, and we are on the same terms as yesterday.*

**"Whaddaya gonna do?"** — The house philosophy: a verbal shrug that converts moral responsibility into weather. Deployed by nearly every character, deliberately.

**"Fuhgeddaboudit"** — Genuine, though the show uses it sparingly and self-consciously, aware it's a cliché.

**"Members Only"** — A jacket brand, an episode title (S6E1), and — in the finale — an unnamed man at a counter. See [The Ending](../the-ending.md).

**"Every day is a gift"** — Tony's post-coma mantra in season six. Lasts about four episodes.

**"It's all a big nothing"** — [Livia's](../characters/livia-soprano.md) doctrine, inherited by [A.J.](../characters/aj-soprano.md)
