## The audition

The best casting story in the series.

Chase saw Van Zandt **inducting The Rascals into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame** in 1997 and was struck by the persona — the delivery, the clothes, the total commitment of the bit. He asked him to audition **for Tony Soprano**.

Van Zandt had never acted in his life.

HBO, reasonably, would not put an untrained musician in the lead of a drama series. Rather than lose him, **Chase wrote a new character specifically for him** — Silvio Dante, consigliere and manager of the Bada Bing, who does not exist in the original pilot script.

---

## Silvio

The performance works because Van Zandt isn't acting so much as inhabiting a very particular register he already owned: the fastidiousness, the deadpan, the hair, the suits, the sense of a man who has thought carefully about his own presentation.

What he adds is stillness. [Silvio](../characters/silvio-dante.md) is the calmest person in every room, which makes him the most frightening — he's the one who kills [Adriana](../characters/adriana-la-cerva.md), without commentary and without needing to discuss it afterward.

He also does the ***Godfather Part III*** impression, on request, for years, always slightly worse. It's the show's neatest joke about itself.

He was never Emmy-nominated.

## Music

He is credited as a music consultant, and Chase occasionally consulted him — though **Chase selected the tracks himself**, with music producer Martin Bruestle and editor Kathryn Dayak. See [Music](../analysis/music.md).

His own musical career is substantial and independent: the E Street Band since the 1970s, a solo career as Little Steven and the Disciples of Soul, and — most consequentially — organizing **Artists United Against Apartheid** and the 1985 "Sun City" single, which had a measurable effect on the cultural boycott of South Africa.

He also created and hosts the long-running radio show *Little Steven's Underground Garage*.

---

## After

He created and starred in ***Lilyhammer*** (2012–14), as a New York mobster relocated to Norway in witness protection — notable as **Netflix's first original series**, predating *House of Cards* by a year.

He returned to touring with Springsteen and published a memoir, *Unrequited Infatuations*, in 2021.
