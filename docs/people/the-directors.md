# The Directors

Chase directed exactly two episodes — the pilot and the finale. Everything in between was a small rotating group with an unusually consistent house style.

---

## The regulars

| Director | Episodes | Notable |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| **Tim Van Patten** | **20** | "Members Only," "Long Term Parking," "Soprano Home Movies" |
| **John Patterson** | 13 | "I Dream of Jeannie Cusamano," "Funhouse," "Whitecaps" |
| **Allen Coulter** | 12 | **"College,"** "Pine Barrens"'s season, "The Test Dream" |
| **Alan Taylor** | 9 | "Kennedy and Heidi," "The Blue Comet," *[Many Saints](../series/the-many-saints-of-newark.md)* |
| **Jack Bender** | 4 | "Mayham" |
| **Henry J. Bronchtein** | 4 | "From Where to Eternity" |
| **Dan Attias** | 3 | "46 Long" |
| **Steve Buscemi** | 4 | **"Pine Barrens"** |
| **David Chase** | **2** | "The Sopranos," **"Made in America"** |

**Tim Van Patten** is the show's most-used director and drew the largest share of its heaviest hours. He went on to *Boardwalk Empire*, *Game of Thrones*, and *The Pacific*.

**John Patterson** directed most of the season finales through the early run and died in 2005, during production; season six's opening credits carry a dedication to him.

**Allen Coulter** directed **"College"** — the episode that established what the show was — and went on to *Boardwalk Empire* and features.

**Alan Taylor** won an Emmy for the show, then directed *Game of Thrones*, *Mad Men*, *Thor: The Dark World*, and [*The Many Saints of Newark*](../series/the-many-saints-of-newark.md).

## The guests

The show periodically hired filmmakers rather than television directors, and the results are among its most distinctive hours:

**Steve Buscemi** directed **"Pine Barrens"** (S3E37) — widely considered one of the greatest television episodes ever made — plus three more, *before* being cast as [Tony Blundetto](../characters/tony-blundetto.md) in season five. Directing his way into an acting role is a genuinely unusual career move.

**Peter Bogdanovich** directed **"Sentimental Education"** (S5E58) while also playing **Dr. Elliot Kupferberg**, [Melfi's](../characters/dr-melfi.md) therapist, across multiple seasons.

**Mike Figgis** (*Leaving Las Vegas*) directed "Cold Cuts" (S5E62).

**Lee Tamahori** (*Once Were Warriors*) directed "Toodle-Fucking-Oo" (S2E16).

**Rodrigo García** (*In Treatment*) directed "All Happy Families…" (S5E56).

**Nick Gomez**, an independent filmmaker whose work [Edie Falco](edie-falco.md) had appeared in, directed "Denial, Anger, Acceptance" (S1E3).

## The house style

Whoever was directing, the show looks consistent, and that's deliberate. Chase's room broke scripts down **shot by shot** before shooting, which pushed the decisions upstream.

The signatures: long takes, static or slow cameras, a lot of medium-wide staging that lets scenes play out in space rather than in cutting, natural light where possible, and a willingness to hold on a face for far longer than television convention allows.

The show also uses **35mm film** and a feature-film aspect ratio throughout, and its cinematographers — **Alik Sakharov** (who went on to *Game of Thrones*) and **Phil Abraham** (who moved into directing, including on *Mad Men*) — light it like a film rather than like television.

That combination is the single most imitated thing about the series' craft. See [Direction & Craft](../analysis/direction-and-craft.md).
