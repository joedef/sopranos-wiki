## Spider

His breakthrough was **Spider in *Goodfellas*** — the young waiter Joe Pesci shoots in the foot for being slow, and then kills.

*The Sopranos* inverts it deliberately: in season one, [Christopher](../characters/christopher-moltisanti.md) shoots a bakery clerk in the foot over a delayed order. Imperioli has confirmed the reference was intentional.

He's one of **27 actors** in both.

---

## Christopher

A five-nomination, one-win run for the show's saddest arc — a man raised by [Tony Soprano](../characters/tony-soprano.md) to be like Tony Soprano, who lacks the constitution and can't be anything else.

The performance's hardest note is the one Imperioli holds for six seasons: Christopher is genuinely dangerous *and* genuinely pitiable, often in the same scene, and never resolves into either.

He won the **2004 Emmy** for Supporting Actor.

---

## The writing

Unusually, he **wrote five episodes** — one of very few actors to write substantially for a show they starred in:

- "From Where to Eternity" (S2E22) — Christopher's near-death vision of hell
- "The Telltale Moozadell" (S3E35)
- "Christopher" (S4E42) — the Columbus Day episode
- "Everybody Hurts" (S4E45) — Artie's suicide attempt
- "Marco Polo" (S5E60)

Chase has said he was a genuinely good writer who happened to also be in the cast.

---

## After

*Life on Mars*, *Detroit 1-8-7*, *Californication*, and then a career-refreshing Emmy-nominated turn in the second season of ***The White Lotus*** (2022) — playing, with some irony, a man on an Italian-heritage pilgrimage.

Since 2020 he has co-hosted ***Talking Sopranos*** with **Steve Schirripa** ([Bobby](../characters/bobby-baccalieri.md)), an episode-by-episode rewatch podcast that became one of the main drivers of the show's streaming-era revival among viewers too young to have watched it first time.

He is also a musician, a practicing Buddhist, and has written a novel.
