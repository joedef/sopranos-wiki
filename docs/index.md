# The Sopranos Wiki

<p class="epigraph">It's good to be in something from the ground floor. I came too late for that, I know. But lately I'm getting the feeling that I came in at the end. The best is over.
<cite>Tony Soprano, in the first therapy session of the first episode</cite></p>

An open, community-maintained reference to **The Sopranos** — David Chase's HBO series, its 86 episodes, the people who made it, and the argument about how it ends.

Two things live here side by side. The first is a **reference work**: every episode with its writer, director, and air date; who's who; what it won; where it was shot. The second is an **analysis** of what the show is actually doing with therapy, with dreams, with food, and with a man who wanted to be told he was a good person by someone qualified to judge.

The short version of why it needs a wiki: *The Sopranos* is a series about a man who goes into therapy to get better and uses it, instead, to get more comfortable. Watching him do that over six seasons — and watching yourself root for him anyway — is the whole thing.
