# Ratings

The show's audience **tripled** between 1999 and 2002 — almost entirely on word of mouth and DVD, in an era before streaming.

## The landmarks

| Episode | Viewers |
| --- | ---: |
| **"For All Debts Public and Private"** (S4E1) — series peak | **13.43M** |
| **"Made in America"** (S6E21) — the finale | **11.90M** |
| **"The Sopranos"** (S1E1) — the pilot | 3.45M |

The finale drew a **49% increase over the previous episode** — its best figure of either half of season six, and the show's largest audience since the season-five premiere. That jump reflects an audience showing up specifically for an ending, which is exactly the appetite Chase then declined to satisfy. See [The Ending](../the-ending.md).

---

## What the shape means

**Season one was small.** HBO in 1999 was a fraction of the size it would be three years later, and 3.46M was a good number for the network and invisible by broadcast standards. The show became a phenomenon *critically* long before it became one commercially.

**The DVD effect.** The gap between season three (spring 2001) and season four (autumn 2002) was 16 months, and it was filled with box sets. A large share of the season-four audience had caught up on DVD — an early demonstration of a model that streaming later industrialized.

**Season five's dip is not decline.** It followed a **21-month gap** after season four. Holding at 9.8M after nearly two years off is unusually strong retention.

**Season six's split cost less than you'd expect.** Twelve episodes in 2006, then a **ten-month gap**, then nine more in 2007. Part two averaged 8.23M against part one's 8.60M — a loss of under 400,000 a week across a ten-month absence, which is remarkable retention rather than a collapse.

**None of this is decline in the normal sense.** The show's lowest-rated season is its first, and the finale is among its most-watched episodes — though the peak remains the season-four premiere at 13.43M.

## The second life

The most interesting ratings story happened long after it ended.

Between roughly **2019 and 2021**, a large new audience found the show — skewing young, driven by HBO Max, the *Talking Sopranos* podcast, the pandemic, a substantial meme ecosystem, and [*The Many Saints of Newark*](../series/the-many-saints-of-newark.md).

HBO reported significant viewership increases across that period, and the show became, for a stretch, one of the most-discussed things on the internet — eighteen years after it premiered. See [After The Sopranos](../series/after-the-sopranos.md).

---

## Caveats

Figures are Nielsen numbers for the **original HBO premiere airing**. HBO also reported same-night repeats and weekly cumulative audiences, which run substantially higher — some sources cite finale figures above 12M using those measures.

Comparisons to broadcast ratings of the era are not like-for-like: HBO was a subscription channel in roughly 25–30 million homes, so a 10M audience represents a far higher share of available viewers than the raw number suggests.
