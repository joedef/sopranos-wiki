## The thesis statements

> **"Lately I'm getting the feeling that I came in at the end. The best is over."**
> — [Tony](../characters/tony-soprano.md), first therapy session, **S1E1**. The show's entire subject, stated in the first ten minutes. See [Themes](../analysis/themes.md).

> **"It's all a big nothing."**
> — [Livia](../characters/livia-soprano.md), to [A.J.](../characters/aj-soprano.md) at a funeral, **S2**. Never refuted by the series.

> **"You probably don't even hear it when it happens."**
> — [Bobby](../characters/bobby-baccalieri.md), on dying, **S6E78 "Soprano Home Movies."** Chase cuts back to it in "The Blue Comet," and a large share of the audience takes it to Holsten's. See [The Ending](../the-ending.md).

> **"Those who want respect, give respect."**
> — Tony, repeatedly, always while demanding it.

## The malapropisms

The show's signature comic register — men reaching for a word above their vocabulary, with nobody present who knows better.

> **"A revolving door of terror."** — Little Carmine

> **"Create a little dysentery among the ranks."** — Christopher

> **"Prostate with grief."**

> **"The sacred and the propane."** — Little Carmine

## Commonly misattributed

**"Leave the gun, take the cannoli."** Not this show. *The Godfather* (1972), improvised by Richard Castellano. It gets attributed to *The Sopranos* constantly because [Silvio](../characters/silvio-dante.md) and the crew quote *Godfather* material so often.

**"Say hello to my little friend."** *Scarface*.

**"Whaddaya gonna do?"** Genuinely in the show, repeatedly — but it isn't a signature line so much as a verbal tic the writers gave to almost everyone, deliberately, as a way of characterizing a culture that treats fatalism as manners.

**"Don't stop believin'."** Journey, 1981. Selected by Tony from a tabletop jukebox in **S6E86**, and cut off mid-word. Chase has said the ceiling he was going for was exactly that phrase, simply and on the nose — which is not the ironic reading most people assume. See [Music](../analysis/music.md).
