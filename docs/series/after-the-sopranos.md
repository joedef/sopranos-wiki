# After The Sopranos

The series ended June 10, 2007. This page covers what came after — the projects, the deaths, and the strange second life the show found on streaming.

---

## The sequel that keeps almost happening

There has never been a *Sopranos* continuation, and there has almost always been talk of one.

**The obstacles are, in order:**

1. **[James Gandolfini died on June 19, 2013**](../people/james-gandolfini.md), of a heart attack in Rome, aged 51. Any continuation of the original story died with him. Chase has been unambiguous that he would not recast Tony.
2. **Chase doesn't want to.** He has spent eighteen years declining to extend, explain, or resolve the ending, and treats requests to do so as a category error.
3. **The prequel underperformed theatrically.** [*The Many Saints of Newark*](the-many-saints-of-newark.md) took $13.1M on a $50M budget in 2021, which cooled studio appetite considerably.

**What's actually been discussed:** Chase has said he'd consider a second film covering **Tony in his twenties** — the gap between *Many Saints* and the series. **Terence Winter** has publicly said he'd do it. Chase has also said Warner Bros. Discovery hasn't made it a priority.

As of now, nothing is in production.

## Where the cast went

**[Edie Falco](../people/edie-falco.md)** went almost straight into ***Nurse Jackie*** (2009–15), winning a fourth Emmy — this time in comedy — for a character about as far from Carmela as the same actress could get.

**[Michael Imperioli](../people/michael-imperioli.md)** did *Life on Mars*, *Detroit 1-8-7*, and then a career-refreshing turn in the second season of ***The White Lotus*** (2022), for which he was Emmy-nominated. He co-hosts ***Talking Sopranos*** with Steve Schirripa, an episode-by-episode rewatch podcast that became one of the biggest drivers of the show's streaming revival.

**[Steven Van Zandt](../people/steven-van-zandt.md)** went back to the E Street Band and made ***Lilyhammer*** (2012–14) — notable as **Netflix's first original series**, predating *House of Cards*.

**[Drea de Matteo](../people/drea-de-matteo.md)** went to *Joey*, *Sons of Anarchy*, and *Desperate Housewives*.

**[Lorraine Bracco](../people/lorraine-bracco.md)** moved largely into unscripted work, including *My Big Italian Adventure*, and wrote about her own long history with therapy.

**[Dominic Chianese](../people/dominic-chianese.md)** continued acting and performing as a singer well into his nineties.

**Robert Iler** and **Jamie-Lynn Sigler** both stepped back from acting substantially; Iler co-hosts the rewatch podcast *Pajama Pants*. See [Iler & Sigler](../people/iler-and-sigler.md).

**Michael K. Williams**, **Steve Buscemi**, and others went on to define the HBO drama era that *The Sopranos* had made possible.

## The losses

The cast has been hit hard. The most significant:

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **Nancy Marchand** (Livia) | June 18, 2000 — during the run, before season three |
| **Joseph Siravo** (Johnny Boy) | April 11, 2021 |
| **James Gandolfini** (Tony) | June 19, 2013, aged 51 |
| **Frank Vincent** (Phil Leotardo) | September 13, 2017 |
| **Tony Sirico** (Paulie Walnuts) | July 8, 2022, aged 79 |
| **Ray Liotta** (*Many Saints*) | May 26, 2022 |
| **Denise Borino-Quinn** (Ginny Sacrimoni) | November 2010, aged 46 |

Gandolfini's death produced a scale of public grief unusual for a television actor, and a genuinely unusual outpouring from New Jersey specifically. He was 51, and had spent the decade after the show deliberately taking small, strange, un-Tony-like parts.

## The streaming revival

Something unexpected happened around 2019–2021: **a large new audience found the show, and it skewed young.**

The drivers, roughly in order:

- **The 20th anniversary in 2019** and the wave of retrospective criticism
- **HBO Max** putting the full run in one accessible place
- ***Talking Sopranos***, the Imperioli/Schirripa rewatch podcast
- **The pandemic**, which was extremely good for six-season dramas
- **A meme ecosystem** on Twitter, TikTok, and Instagram that turned the show into a shared vocabulary for a generation that was in primary school when it aired
- *The Many Saints of Newark* driving a measurable spike

The effect was that a show which ended in 2007 became, for a stretch, one of the most-discussed things on the internet — and that its reputation *rose*, which almost never happens to prestige television from that era.

## What it left behind

The short version is on [Legacy & Influence](../reference/legacy-and-influence.md). The one-line version: *The Sopranos* is the reason television drama stopped being a lesser form, and the reason a protagonist no longer has to be likeable.

Every antihero drama of the following two decades — *The Wire*, *Deadwood*, *Mad Men*, *Breaking Bad*, *The Shield*, *Boardwalk Empire*, *Better Call Saul*, *Succession* — is downstream of a 1999 argument about whether a lead character could murder someone in episode five.
