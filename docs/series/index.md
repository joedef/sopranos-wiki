## The shape of the run

**Seasons 1–2** build the machine. The premise — mob boss in therapy — is doing double duty as exposition, and Chase uses [Melfi](../characters/dr-melfi.md) to explain a world he'd otherwise have to stop and explain. The [Big Pussy](../characters/big-pussy.md) arc gives season 2 a spine that season 1 doesn't quite have.

**Season 3** is where it becomes a novel. [Livia](../characters/livia-soprano.md) dies (of necessity — [Nancy Marchand](../people/nancy-marchand.md) had died in June 2000), and the show loses the antagonist it was designed around. What replaces her is diffuse and worse for Tony: his children growing up, his own appetites, and [Ralph](../characters/ralph-cifaretto.md).

**Season 4** is the most divisive and, on rewatch, among the best. Almost nothing happens for eleven episodes — the earners aren't earning, the schemes are small and grubby — and then "Whitecaps" burns the marriage down. It's a season about money and it's structured like one.

**Season 5** is the most conventionally satisfying: a clean premise (men released from long sentences trying to re-enter a business that moved on), a great guest performance from Steve Buscemi as [Tony Blundetto](../characters/tony-blundetto.md), and "Long Term Parking."

**Season 6** is the argument. Part one puts Tony in a coma and gives him an alternate life; part two systematically removes every reason to hope he learned anything. It contains the show's most experimental hour and its most brutal, and then it stops mid-sentence.

## Who made them

The writers' room was small and stayed small. **David Chase** wrote or co-wrote 2–7 episodes a season and rewrote nearly everything else. **Robin Green** and **Mitchell Burgess** came with him from *Northern Exposure*. **Terence Winter** joined in season 2 and became the show's other defining voice. **Matthew Weiner** was hired in season 5 after Chase read his *Mad Men* pilot script.

On the directing side, **Tim Van Patten** did the most (20 episodes), followed by **John Patterson** (13), **Allen Coulter** (12), and **Alan Taylor** (9). Chase directed exactly two: the pilot and the finale.

Notable one-offs: **Steve Buscemi** directed "Pine Barrens" before he was cast in the show. **Peter Bogdanovich** directed "Sentimental Education" while also playing Melfi's own therapist. **Mike Figgis**, **Lee Tamahori**, and **Rodrigo García** each took an episode.
