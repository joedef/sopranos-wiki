## The character

Bobby starts as [Junior's](junior-soprano.md) driver and caretaker: gentle, devoted, hopeless at the parts of the job that require menace, and mocked constantly by the crew for his weight and his hobbies.

He collects **model trains**. He loves his wife. He is a genuinely present father. He is, by a distance, the most emotionally healthy adult in the organization, and everybody finds this faintly embarrassing.

Uniquely among the main crew, he had **never killed anyone** until season six — a fact the show treats as both his defining virtue and the thing that makes him a curiosity to the others.

---

## Janice

After Karen dies, [Janice](janice-soprano.md) moves in on him with a directness that is genuinely uncomfortable to watch — including the scene in which she gets him to eat his dead wife's last frozen ziti.

She marries him and, across four seasons, steadily makes him worse: more ambitious, more compromised, and eventually a killer.

---

## The lake house

**"Soprano Home Movies"** (S6E78) is the character's best hour and the show's most efficient piece of writing about Tony.

Tony and Bobby, drunk, playing Monopoly at a lake house, get into an argument that turns into a real fistfight — and **Bobby wins**. He's bigger, and Tony is out of shape, and it's over quickly and completely.

Tony cannot let it stand. Within days he sends Bobby to Canada to commit his first murder — a debt collection that requires him to shoot a man in front of a woman with a baby.

Bobby does it, and comes back changed, and Tony is satisfied. It's the pettiest thing Tony does in the series and it costs a man his soul.

Earlier in the same episode, discussing what dying is like, Bobby offers the line the show returns to in "The Blue Comet" and that a large portion of the audience takes to Holsten's: *you probably don't even hear it when it happens.*

---

## The train shop

In **"The Blue Comet,"** Bobby is buying a model train — the Blue Comet, a real Jersey Central line — when two Lupertazzi gunmen come into the shop.

He is shot repeatedly and falls into the display, and the trains keep running over him.

It's the death that hurts most after [Adriana's](adriana-la-cerva.md), for the same reason: the show kills the person with the least appetite for any of it, doing the thing that made him happy.

## Steve Schirripa

A former Las Vegas casino executive — entertainment director at the Riviera — with no acting background before the series.

Since 2020 he has co-hosted ***Talking Sopranos*** with [Michael Imperioli](../people/michael-imperioli.md), one of the main engines of the show's streaming-era revival.
