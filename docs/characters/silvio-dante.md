The quietest member of the inner circle, and the one who does the things nobody wants to discuss.

---

## The role

**Consigliere** — counselor to the boss, outside the chain of command. Silvio is exactly right for it: he has no ambition to be boss, no interest in a crew of his own, and gives advice that is consistently sound and consistently ignored.

He runs the **Bada Bing**, which is the crew's office as much as it is a strip club, and manages it with the fussy competence of a small-business owner. A great deal of the series takes place in its back room.

He is also, quietly, the one who kills people. [Adriana](adriana-la-cerva.md) is his. When something has to be done and cannot be delegated, Tony sends Silvio, because Silvio does it without commentary, without enjoying it, and without needing to talk about it afterward.

That combination — mild, reasonable, entirely without hesitation — is more disturbing than [Paulie's](paulie-walnuts.md) volatility or [Ralph's](ralph-cifaretto.md) sadism.

---

## The bit

His *Godfather Part III* impression — *just when I thought I was out, they pull me back in* — is done on request, repeatedly, for years, and always slightly worse than the last time.

It's the show's cleanest joke about itself: a fictional mobster doing a bad impression of a fictional mobster, for other fictional mobsters who find it hilarious, in a series about men who learned how to be gangsters from movies. See [Mob Movies & Self-Awareness](../analysis/mob-movies-and-self-awareness.md).

## The end

In **"The Blue Comet"** (S6E85), Silvio and Patsy are ambushed outside the Bing. Patsy escapes. Silvio is shot several times.

He survives, in a coma, and Tony visits him in hospital in the finale. The doctors don't expect him to wake up. Tony sits with him briefly and can't find anything to say.

Losing Silvio is the practical reason Tony is so exposed by the last episode: the man who managed everything is gone, [Bobby](bobby-baccalieri.md) is dead, [Christopher](christopher-moltisanti.md) is dead, and what's left is [Paulie](paulie-walnuts.md).

## Steven Van Zandt

The casting is one of the show's great flukes. **[Van Zandt](../people/steven-van-zandt.md)** — Bruce Springsteen's guitarist, of the E Street Band — had **never acted**. Chase saw him inducting The Rascals into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, was struck by the persona, and asked him to audition **for Tony**.

HBO wanted a trained actor in the lead. Rather than lose him, Chase **wrote the character of Silvio Dante specifically for him**.

Van Zandt also contributed to the show's music decisions, though Chase selected the tracks himself. He went on to create and star in ***Lilyhammer*** (2012), which was **Netflix's first original series**.
